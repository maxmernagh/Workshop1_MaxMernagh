THE OG STORY

Told by OG Anunoby.



But first, here are 10 quick OG facts

  1. His full name is Ogugua Anunoby Jr.
  2. He was born in London, England, on July 17th, 1997, but grew up in Jefferson City, Missouri, USA
  3. His official height and weight is 6 ft 7 in and 232 lb
  4. His official wingspan is 7 feet 3 in
  5. He went to Jefferson City High School, Missouri (2011/15)
  6. He played D1 Basketball for the Indiana Hoosier's at Indiana University Bloomington (2015/17)
  7. He was drafted 23rd overall in the 2017 NBA Draft by the Toronto Raptors and has become vital to their success
  8. He mostly plays the small-forward and power-forward positions
  9. He wears #3 in honour of Allen Iverson
  10. He is a fashion trendsetter, self-proclaimed best dancer on the Raptors, and an avid Young Thug listener

OG's Story in OG's Style

I was born in London, England, but moved to Jefferson City, Missouri, USA, with my family when I was 4. So I really don't remember my time in England at all, but I plan to visit there a lot more soon. Growing up was pretty cool. I studied hard, played soccer, football, baseball, and basketball. Everyone thought I was best at baseball, but I loved basketball so I stuck with it. In High School I played for the Jefferson City Jays at Jefferson City High School. It was cool because we were a good team and I had fun with all my teammates. I also played AAU which really helped my development. In my final year of high school I was ranked pretty low, but that was fine because everything turned out better than I imagained it. I decided to go to Indiana University Bloomington and play for the Indiana Hoosier's. I was roommates with Thomas Bryant who is on the Washington Wizards now. I came off the bench for the Hoosier's in my freshman year, but I played good. I focused on defense and I had some really nice plays that year (2015/16). In my sophomore year I think I played only 12 games because I tore my ACL after grabbing a rebound, and ended up having to get surgery in New York about a month later. After the surgery I decided to just focus on getting prepared for the 2017 NBA Draft, but since I was able to move around much it was pretty difficult, but I got through it. I just watched film of myself and scouted everything I did good and bad, and then worked on those things. My body healed pretty fast, but I still wasn't at 100% during the draft.


"With the 23rd pick in the 2017 NBA Draft, the Toronto Raptors select OG Anunoby."





I think I could have been drafted higher for sure, but I wouldn't go back and change that. After my meeting and workout with the Raptors about a month before the draft, we both had mutual interest with eachother. I love the Raptors front office and the city, and I knew I would learn a lot from all the players and the training staff. I'm super hyped that the Raptors drafted me, I love our team. I was still injured when I got drafted, but a few months later at training camp I was finally cleared to play; I was 2 months ahead of schedule for my recovery. I signed my rookie contract on November 14th, 2017, and I was really lucky in my rookie year because I was able to be the starting small-forward and I learnt a lot from Kyle, Demar, Freddy, Pascal, and literally everyone else taught me something valuable.

In my second year we got Kawhi Leonard, and he is a small-forward also, so I came off the bench that year. I'm a team player so I had no issue with that, and Kawhi taught me a lot about how to work out how to be better defensively. I trained with him a lot. He's a fun guy for sure. I had an emergency appendectomy at the beginning of the playoffs that year as well, which really sucked because I pretty much missed all of our championship run. I was with team during the NBA Finals, but didn't play. I still had fun though. I'm proud of they guys for winning the championship, but yah I wish I could have played. Celebrating with the team and staff was a moment I'll never forget. The parade was crazy; there was so much people. I was pretty lit to be honest, but not as much as Marc Gasol or Norman Powell.

7.0 2.9 0.7 20.2 67

I was a starter again in my third year (2019/20) because Kawhi Leonard decided to leave our team. That's fine, he can do what he wants. I was confident we could repeat as NBA Champs without him though. The season was going well for us. Pascal and Fred were killing it, and then COVID-19 shut the league down for a few months. Shortly after the league shutdown, we went into lockdown. It was hard to train during the lockdown but I tried my best to stay in shape. When we got to the NBA Restart at the Orlando Bubble I was still confident as ever in our team to win the championship, but we lost to the Boston Celtics. I did hit a nasty buzzer-beater over Jaylen Brown in Game 3 after being down by 2 games. We ended up going to Game 7, and we could have won the series but Boston just got the better of us.


"Series on the line, Anunoby got it off.. And it goes!"





I had hit buzzer-beater's before in highschool, so I wasn't surprised that it went in. It was cool though. I wouldn't have been able to hit that shot though if it weren't for my teammates and coaches always encouraging me to shoot. I stay cool through moments like that because I always expect to make the play. I don't shoot trying to miss. Thinking about it now though, it was pretty crazy how I was able to get the shot off in 0.3 seconds from 3-point-range. I'm happy I was able to do taht for the fans watching all around the world. During the off-season I really focused on getting stronger but faster, I didn't want to gain any more weight but I wanted to have more muscle mass and increased stamina and agility. In every off-season I focus on the part of my game that needs it most.

I signed a 4-year extension with the Raptors before the 2020/2021 season, and I am excited for my role to increase every year. I think our future as a team is very bright. All of my teammates are playing great and getting better everyday in practice. I try to share defensive tips with everyone, and I want to become more of a leader for this team. We have accomplished so much already as a young team, and I know we will be champions again soon. That's my life story so far, and I'll share more with everyone whenever some more cool stuff happens.
